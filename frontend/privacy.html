Overview

Robin is a personal AI assistant that connects to your Gmail account to summarise emails, draft replies, and prepare follow-ups. Robin only acts with your approval and processes data strictly to deliver these features.

Lawful Basis for Processing

Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and UK GDPR, Robin processes data based on:

  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a)) — when you connect your Gmail account and grant permissions
  • Legitimate interest (Article 6(1)(f)) — to maintain and improve core service functionality (limited and proportionate)

You can withdraw consent at any time by disconnecting Gmail.

Data We Collect

  • Email content: message body, subject, attachments (only when required)
  • Email metadata: sender, recipient, timestamps, thread structure
  • Account data: name, email address, authentication tokens
  • Usage data: actions you approve (e.g. draft sent, follow-up created)

Robin does not access or process data beyond what is required for these functions.

How We Use Data

  • Summarise unread emails
  • Draft replies (for your approval)
  • Identify urgent messages
  • Prepare follow-ups

No email is sent without your explicit approval.

Data Storage & Security

  • Data is stored in Neon (PostgreSQL), hosted in US East
  • Encrypted in transit (TLS) and at rest
  • Access is restricted using least-privilege principles
  • Authentication tokens are securely stored and scoped
  • Logging and monitoring are in place to detect misuse

Access to Data

  • Only you can access your data
  • Robin does not sell or share data with third parties
  • Internal access is strictly limited for maintenance and security

Data Retention

  • Data is retained only while your account is active
  • If you disconnect Gmail or delete your account:
    • Access tokens are revoked immediately
    • Email data is deleted within 30 days
  • Minimal temporary processing may occur in memory for real-time features

Your Rights

Under GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access your data
  • Correct inaccurate data
  • Request deletion
  • Restrict processing
  • Data portability
  • Object to processing
  • Lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)

Requests can be made via: privacy@robinapp.io

Third-Party Processors

Robin uses the following processors:

  • Google — Gmail API (email access)
  • Neon — PostgreSQL database hosting
  • Render — Application hosting
  • Anthropic — AI inference (Claude API)
  • Twilio — WhatsApp and SMS messaging

All processors operate under Data Processing Agreements (DPAs).

International Data Transfers

If data is transferred outside the UK/EU, safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) are used to ensure compliance.

Automated Decision-Making

Robin does not make automated decisions with legal or significant effects. All actions (e.g. sending emails) require user approval.

Data Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach, affected users will be notified in accordance with GDPR requirements (within 72 hours of becoming aware).

Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Users will be notified of material changes via WhatsApp or email.
