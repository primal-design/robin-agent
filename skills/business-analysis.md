# Business Analysis Skill

## When to use
When the user wants to analyse a business idea, validate a concept, understand their market, or do any strategic business research.

## How to run the analysis

Work through these layers IN ORDER. After each layer give Robin one sharp insight, then move to the next. Never dump everything at once.

### Layer 1 — The Idea (30 seconds)
Ask: What is it? Who buys it? What problem does it solve?
If they can't answer in 2 sentences — the idea needs sharpening first.

### Layer 2 — Demand Check
Use research tool:
- Google Trends for the core keyword (growing/flat/dying?)
- Search volume + intent (are people actively looking?)
- Reddit/Facebook — are people complaining about this problem?
- TikTok/Instagram — is content about this getting engagement?

Signal: if people are already paying for a bad version of this → strong demand.

### Layer 3 — Market Size (TAM/SAM/SOM)
- TAM: total global market
- SAM: your reachable segment (geography, niche)
- SOM: your realistic Year 1 slice (usually 0.1–1% of SAM)
If SOM < £50k/year → too small unless it's a stepping stone.

### Layer 4 — Competition (Porter's Five Forces)
Research top 3–5 competitors:
- What do they charge?
- What do reviews say they're bad at? (that's your gap)
- How strong is their brand?
- How easy is it for someone to copy you?
- Is there a dominant player? (means market is validated, not dead)

### Layer 5 — PESTEL
Quick scan for external threats:
- Political: any regulation risk?
- Economic: recession-proof or luxury?
- Social: growing trend or fading?
- Tech: will AI/automation kill this in 2 years?
- Environmental: any sustainability angle or risk?
- Legal: licences, GDPR, IP issues?

### Layer 6 — SWOT
Based on everything above:
- Strengths: what the founder uniquely has
- Weaknesses: honest gaps (skills, capital, network)
- Opportunities: specific gaps in the market
- Threats: the 2–3 things that could kill this

### Layer 7 — ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
Be specific:
- Age, job, income level
- What do they google at 2am?
- Where do they hang out online?
- What have they already tried and failed?
- What would make them pay TODAY vs "maybe later"?

### Layer 8 — Unit Economics
- What's the price point?
- Cost to deliver (COGS)?
- Gross margin = (Price - COGS) / Price — needs to be >50% for services, >30% for products
- CAC estimate: how much to acquire one customer?
- LTV: how much does one customer spend over their lifetime?
- LTV:CAC ratio needs to be >3x to be viable

### Layer 9 — Validation Plan
Don't build until validated. Cheapest tests first:
1. Landing page with email capture — does anyone sign up?
2. Manual service first — do it by hand before automating
3. Pre-sale — can you get 5 people to pay before you build?
4. Competitor gap — are people already complaining about the market leader?

### Layer 10 — Keywords & Content Gap
Research:
- Top 5 keywords in this niche (volume + difficulty)
- What content ranks? Blogs, videos, communities?
- Meta Ads Library — what ads are competitors running?
- X/Twitter — what conversations are happening?

## Output format
After running all layers, give Robin one final verdict:

**GO** — strong demand, clear gap, viable unit economics, founder has edge
**GO WITH CHANGES** — good core but needs pivoting on [specific thing]
**VALIDATE FIRST** — uncertain demand, needs a test before investing time/money
**STOP** — market too small, too competitive, or regulatory risk too high

Always end with: "Here's your ONE next step: [specific action]"

## Constraints
- Never give generic advice — always tie it to their specific idea
- If you don't have enough info to run a layer, ask ONE question to get it
- Flag anything that looks like a serious risk early — don't bury it
- Be honest — a bad idea told early saves months of wasted work
