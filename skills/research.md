# Research Skill

## When to use
Use this skill whenever the user asks to research anything — a person, a company, a market, a topic, or a trend.

## How to think before acting
1. Read the request carefully — what TYPE of research is needed?
   - **person** → individual (competitor, client, influencer, founder)
   - **market** → industry, niche, or opportunity space
   - **topic** → concept, strategy, how-to, or question
   - **competitor** → a specific business or product
   - **trend** → what's hot, growing, or emerging right now

2. Ask yourself: what does this person actually NEED from this research?
   - Are they trying to reach someone? → focus on contact/background
   - Are they entering a market? → focus on opportunity/gaps
   - Are they comparing options? → focus on pros/cons/pricing

3. Run the research tool with the right type and a focused query.

## Output format
- Lead with the 3 most important insights
- Use bullet points
- End with one "so what" — what should the user DO with this info

## Constraints
- Never fabricate data points — if unsure, say so
- Flag anything that seems outdated
